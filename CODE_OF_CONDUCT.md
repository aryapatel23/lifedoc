# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of LifeDoc pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and professional environment where diverse perspectives are valued and respected, especially given the sensitive nature of healthcare technology.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat everyone with dignity and respect, recognizing healthcare is personal
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and health experiences
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together towards improving healthcare technology
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional communication, especially regarding health topics
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and accept it gracefully
- **Show Empathy**: Consider others' health situations and perspectives
- **Give Credit**: Acknowledge others' contributions and research
- **Stay Focused**: Keep discussions relevant to LifeDoc's mission
- **Protect Privacy**: Never share real patient data or personal health information

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome comments, personal attacks, or intimidation
- **Discrimination**: Prejudiced actions based on health status, disability, or protected characteristics
- **Health Shaming**: Judging or mocking anyone's health conditions or choices
- **Medical Advice**: Providing unauthorized medical advice or diagnosis
- **Privacy Violations**: Sharing real patient data, PHI, or personal health information
- **Trolling**: Deliberately inflammatory or off-topic comments
- **Doxxing**: Publishing others' private information without consent
- **Spam**: Excessive self-promotion or irrelevant content
- **Disrespect**: Dismissive comments about others' skills, health, or contributions
- **Misinformation**: Deliberately spreading false health information
- **Inappropriate Content**: Sexual, violent, or otherwise offensive material

## Project-Specific Guidelines

Given that LifeDoc is a healthcare management platform dealing with sensitive health data:

### Healthcare Privacy & Ethics

- **HIPAA Awareness**: Respect healthcare privacy standards even in development
- **No Real Data**: Always use dummy/seed data - NEVER real patient information
- **Confidentiality**: Don't discuss real health cases or identifiable information
- **Test Data Only**: Use synthetic test data in all examples and discussions
- **Professional Standards**: Follow healthcare software development best practices
- **Security First**: Prioritize data security in all contributions

### Medical Sensitivity

- **Be Compassionate**: Health discussions can be sensitive and emotional
- **No Medical Advice**: Contributors should not provide medical advice through the platform
- **Respect Experiences**: People have diverse health experiences; be understanding
- **Mental Health**: Be mindful that health challenges affect mental wellbeing
- **Accessibility**: Consider users with disabilities in design and development
- **Cultural Respect**: Healthcare practices vary by culture; be respectful

### Technical Discussions

- **Focus on Code**: Keep technical debates professional, not personal
- **Explain Clearly**: Not everyone has medical or technical backgrounds
- **Assume Good Intent**: People may make mistakes; respond constructively
- **Document Decisions**: Explain reasoning behind healthcare-related technical choices
- **Security Standards**: Take security vulnerabilities seriously and report privately
- **Quality First**: Healthcare software requires high quality and reliability

### Cultural & Accessibility

- **Global Perspective**: Healthcare practices differ worldwide; be inclusive
- **Language**: English is primary, but be patient with non-native speakers
- **Time Zones**: Contributors span multiple time zones; be understanding
- **Accessibility**: Design for people with various abilities and health conditions
- **Health Literacy**: Use clear language; avoid unnecessary medical jargon

## Responsibilities

### Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that violate this Code
- Protecting user privacy and health data security
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate
- Setting a positive example in their own behavior
- Ensuring healthcare ethics are maintained

### Contributors

All contributors are responsible for:

- Reading and following this Code of Conduct
- Reporting violations they witness or experience
- Protecting patient privacy and health data
- Supporting others who are being treated poorly
- Maintaining professional healthcare standards
- Helping create a safe, welcoming environment

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

1. **GitHub Issues**: For public discussions (non-sensitive matters)
2. **Email**: [Maintainer email - to be added]
3. **Private Message**: Contact @[maintainer-username] directly on GitHub
4. **Security Issues**: Use GitHub Security Advisories for vulnerabilities

All reports will be handled confidentially and with sensitivity to healthcare privacy concerns.

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

- Your contact information (or report anonymously)
- Names/handles of people involved
- Description of the incident
- Context and any relevant screenshots/logs (redact any health data)
- Whether the incident is ongoing
- Impact on you or others
- Any other relevant information

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: Reports acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Confidential Review**: Maintainers review privately
3. **Investigation**: May contact involved parties for clarification
4. **Decision**: Maintainers determine appropriate action
5. **Implementation**: Action taken to resolve situation
6. **Follow-up**: Reporter informed of outcome (if not anonymous)
7. **Documentation**: Incident documented privately for pattern tracking

### Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity and context:

#### 1. Correction
- **Trigger**: First-time minor offense or misunderstanding
- **Action**: Private message with explanation and guidance
- **Goal**: Education and behavior improvement
- **Follow-up**: No further action if behavior corrects

#### 2. Warning
- **Trigger**: Repeated minor offense or moderate violation
- **Action**: Formal written warning with consequences explanation
- **Impact**: Documented in private maintainer records
- **Follow-up**: Monitoring of future behavior

#### 3. Temporary Ban
- **Trigger**: Serious violation or pattern of bad behavior
- **Action**: Temporary suspension from all project spaces
- **Duration**: Typically 7-90 days depending on severity
- **Conditions**: Must acknowledge violation and commit to improvement
- **Reinstatement**: After demonstrating understanding and changed behavior

#### 4. Permanent Ban
- **Trigger**: Severe violations, especially privacy/security breaches
- **Action**: Permanent removal from all project spaces
- **Scope**: GitHub, communication channels, events, etc.
- **Examples**: Sharing real patient data, severe harassment, security sabotage
- **Appeals**: May be submitted after 12 months with evidence of rehabilitation

### Special Circumstances

#### Healthcare Data Breaches
- **Immediate Action**: Instant removal of exposed data
- **Investigation**: Determine if breach was accidental or malicious
- **Consequences**: Automatic temporary ban pending investigation
- **Reporting**: May require external reporting per regulations

#### Security Vulnerabilities
- **Responsible Disclosure**: Use GitHub Security Advisories
- **No Public Disclosure**: Don't share vulnerabilities publicly before patches
- **Recognition**: Security researchers acknowledged in SECURITY.md
- **No Penalties**: Good-faith security research is protected

### Factors Considered

When determining enforcement action:

- Severity and impact of the behavior
- Whether health data or privacy was compromised
- Intent (accidental mistake vs. malicious action)
- First offense vs. repeated pattern
- Willingness to acknowledge and correct behavior
- Impact on community safety and trust
- History of positive contributions

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- **GitHub**: Repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, releases
- **Communication**: Email, Discord, Slack, chat platforms
- **Events**: Meetups, conferences, workshops (virtual or in-person)
- **Social Media**: When representing LifeDoc publicly
- **Development**: Code reviews, documentation, testing
- **Support**: User support channels and forums

The Code also applies when representing LifeDoc in other communities.

## Healthcare Software Ethics

As a healthcare technology project, we commit to:

### Patient Safety First
- Code quality that protects patient safety
- Rigorous testing for health-critical features
- Clear documentation for medical functionality
- Transparent about limitations and risks

### Privacy by Design
- Privacy-first architecture and development
- Minimal data collection principles
- Strong encryption and security measures
- Regular security audits and updates

### Accessibility
- Design for users of all abilities
- Support for assistive technologies
- Clear, understandable user interfaces
- Multiple language support (where possible)

### Evidence-Based Development
- Features based on healthcare standards
- Integration with medical best practices
- Consultation with healthcare professionals
- Regular updates to reflect medical advances

### Transparency
- Open source code for community review
- Clear about AI/ML model limitations
- Honest about what the system can/cannot do
- Regular security and privacy disclosures
